1. What is a hedgehog?

Hedgehogs are small, spiny mammals that first evolved about 15 million years ago. They are about the size of a adult human hand and carry pointy keratin spikes on their backs. There is a common misconception that Hedgehogs and Porcupines are the same creature but there is a large size difference and a porcupine's quills are much larger. Hedgehogs often curl into a ball when feeling threatened and hope that predators will leave them alone or damage themselves on its quills.   • Caterpillars
  • Earwigs

3. Anointing

On the topic of food hedgehogs are considered to be self-anointing animals. When a hedgehog anoints they foam at the mouth and use their tongue to spread the foam all over their body. Many people may believe that this is abnormal behaviour but it is completely natural. In the wild Hedgehogs are very careful of their scents and try to blend in as much as possible to be able to avoid predators so spreading the scent or a bug or another thing on them will help them blend in more with the smells that surround them.

4. Why are Hedgehogs Nocturnal?

When an animal is nocturnal it means that they are active during the night and sleeping through the day. Most animals have their specific reason for this habit but it is mostly for the sake of survival. Hedgehogs do this because they have less competition to get food during the night time. Hedgehogs have poor eyesight especially when it is light out so they make up for it with their amazing hearing and sense of smell. With their heightened senses they are able to detect if there is other animals around them and use the quills to help.

Overall Hedgehogs are often not awake during the day but there can be an exception to this when a pregnant female is gathering nesting materials before she gives birth or when she is gathering food and water for her young while they sleep during the day. Another exception is when a hedgehog is in danger or does not feel safe enough to sleep during the night.

5. The Life Cycle of a Wild Hedgehog

Hoglets

This is the first stage of a Hedgehogs life because it is the first couple weeks that they are alive. In these weeks they are with their mother who feeds them and makes sure that they are healthy. After these weeks the mother usually abandons them and leaves them to survive on their own. Occasionally a mother may stay in her nest and expect the hoglets to leave but if the mother senses danger at anytime she may eat her own babies. Scientists believe that mother hedgehogs may do this because they fear for the safety and thinks that it is better for her to have all of the nutrients of her young that a predator having it.

Adulthood

This is the majority of a hedgehogs life as they find ways to get food and try to survive in the wild. Hedgehogs do hibernate in the winter so they spend their entire summers eating food and then rest. Since hedgehogs usually live in warmer climates it is important for them to not go through harsh winters as they do not have a ton of fur on them.

Death

Alike to every other living thing on earth, hedgehogs die. Often the life expectancy of a hedgehog is 3-4 years but it can depend on the type of environment that they live in and if they catch any diseases.

Examples of diseases:

  • Eye Problems
  • Wobbly Hedgehog Syndrome
  • Cancer
  • Tooth Pathology
  • Heart Disease
  • Ringworms

6. Hedgehogs as Pets

Hedgehogs have become a common pet for families to have. Since hedgehogs are quiet and low-maintenance animals they can often fit well into a household. When comfortable, hedgehogs are social creatures that enjoy being held and adventuring different areas.

If you are wanting to have a pet hedgehogs there are some things to consider before getting one:

  • They are nocturnal and will be active at night so if its cage is in your bedroom are you fine with the noise?
  • Are you able to provide it with a large enough cage which contains food, water, shelter, toys, a running wheel and more?
  • Will you be willing to bathe and clip the nail of the hedgehog because they will not be able to naturally keep their nails short?
  • They are social but also like their space so will you be willing to provide them a quiet space during the day while they sleep?
  • Are there other animals in the household which may scare the hedgehog or make it uncomfortable?
  • If you plan to take a vacation or go away for a couple days will you have someone who is able to look after the hedgehog?

There is a lot of questions that you may ask yourself before getting a hedgehog but overall they are very lovely creatures to take care and there are many fun things to do with your spikey pet.
